Electron/Hadron Separation Using ML & BDT Results

Variables

  • n0: Number of hit PMTs in the RICH in the expected range of a Cherenkov ring.
  • disp: Distance between a track projection and its associated ring center in the RICH.
  • χ2/npe0: A χ2-like shape variable of the RICH ring associated with the track per npe0, the number of photoelectrons measured in the ring.
  • emcdz: Distance between the track projection point onto the EMCal and the associated EMCal cluster in the z-direction, measured in cm.
  • emcdphi: Distance between the track projection point onto the EMCal and the associated EMCal cluster in the azimuthal direction, measured in radians.
  • prob: Probability that the EMCal cluster is of electromagnetic origin, based on the shower shape.
  • E/p: Variable quantifying the energy-momentum matching for electrons. It is defined as E/p, where E is the energy measured by the EMCal, p is the track momentum, and E/p is expected to be near 1 for electrons.
  • m2(PbSc) and m2(TOF-E): Mass squared calculated using TOF measurements from the TOF-E and PbSc detectors, initial time using BBC, and momentum and path length using DC. The mass squared is calculated as:
    m2 = (p2/c2) * ((c * t / L)2 - 1),
    where p is the momentum, t is the time of flight, L is the path length, and c is the speed of light.

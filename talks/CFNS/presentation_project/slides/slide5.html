Real Data





  • 3D histograms are created to analyze the relationship between:
    • The difference in azimuthal angle (Δφ) between the track trajectory and hit position in the current layer.
    • The Δφ in the previous layer.
    • The transverse momentum (pT) of the track.
  • For each pT bin, the Δφ distribution in the current layer is fitted with a Gaussian function to extract the mean and standard deviation.
  • The same procedure is applied for the polar angle difference (Δθ).
  • The mean and standard deviation are parameterized as functions of pT and Δφ in the previous layer, enabling precise determination of search windows.
  • This method improves the resolution in the innermost layer by a factor of 5 in Δφ, leading to more accurate hit-to-track associations.
